## How OTel logs are stored on disk [\#](/blog/elasticsearch-log-analytics-clickhouse#how-otel-logs-are-stored-on-disk)

To better understand the storage footprint results in the next section, we first look at how Elasticsearch and ClickHouse store and compress ingested OTel logs on disk. The following diagram provides a simplified sketch.

![Blog-FTS-vs-Elasticsearch.001.png](/uploads/Blog_FTS_vs_Elasticsearch_001_19f2e37110.png)
In both systems, ingested OTel log data is ultimately written into immutable on\-disk units:

- [Lucene segments](https://lucene.apache.org/core/3_0_3/fileformats.html#Segments) in Elasticsearch and
- [data parts](https://clickhouse.com/docs/parts) in ClickHouse

These units logically belong to shards (not shown in the diagram) and are continuously merged in the background into larger ones. Segments typically grow to about 5 GB and parts to about 150 GB, though larger forced merges are possible in both systems.

In Elasticsearch, shards in turn make up indexes, as described [here](https://github.com/ClickHouse/examples/tree/main/blog-examples/clickhouse-vs-elasticsearch/on-disk-format-and-insert-processing#elasticsearch); ClickHouse shards are described [here](https://github.com/ClickHouse/examples/tree/main/blog-examples/clickhouse-vs-elasticsearch/on-disk-format-and-insert-processing#clickhouse).

Within those on\-disk units, both systems organize data into specialized structures that serve four main purposes: fast aggregations, full\-text search, returning original documents, and pruning irrelevant data during query execution.

### Columnar storage for fast aggregations [\#](/blog/elasticsearch-log-analytics-clickhouse#columnar-storage-for-fast-aggregations)

Both systems use **① columnar storage**: aggregation queries read only the referenced columns, scan fewer bytes due to per\-column compression, and execute vectorized (SIMD) operations on contiguous data.

In Elasticsearch **[doc\_values](https://www.elastic.co/docs/reference/elasticsearch/mapping-reference/doc-values)** each column [is](https://lucene.apache.org/core/9_9_0/core/org/apache/lucene/codecs/lucene90/Lucene90DocValuesFormat.html) automatically encoded individually with specialized codecs such as delta and gcd, depending on data type and cardinality. However, [no](https://github.com/elastic/elasticsearch/issues/78266) general\-purpose compression algorithm such as lz4 or zstd is applied.

In ClickHouse, compression codec(s) are also applied per **[column file](https://clickhouse.com/docs/parts)**. ClickHouse supports [general\-purpose](https://clickhouse.com/docs/en/sql-reference/statements/create/table#general-purpose-codecs) (e.g. lz4 and zstd), [specialized](https://clickhouse.com/docs/en/sql-reference/statements/create/table#specialized-codecs) (e.g. delta and gcd), and [encryption](https://clickhouse.com/docs/en/sql-reference/statements/create/table#encryption-codecs) codecs (e.g. AES\_128\), which can also be [chained](https://clickhouse.com/blog/optimize-clickhouse-codecs-compression-schema) per column.
