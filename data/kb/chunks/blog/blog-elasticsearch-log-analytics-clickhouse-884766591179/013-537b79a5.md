> **Cold queries are not an edge case in observability.**  
> Engineers often investigate newly emerging incidents, jump to previously unseen time ranges, or search older retained logs. In those situations, warm caches cannot be assumed.

Overall, ClickHouse completes the full cold query suite substantially faster than Elasticsearch across all three scales.

- **1B:** ClickHouse **[1\.54s](https://github.com/ClickHouse/TextBench/blob/main/clickhouse/results/m6i.8xlarge_1b.json)** vs Elasticsearch **[9\.55s](https://github.com/ClickHouse/TextBench/blob/main/elasticsearch/results/m6i.8xlarge_otel_logs_1b.json)** → **6\.20× faster in ClickHouse**
- **10B:** ClickHouse **[7\.41s](https://github.com/ClickHouse/TextBench/blob/main/clickhouse/results/m6i.8xlarge_10b.json)** vs Elasticsearch **[30\.6s](https://github.com/ClickHouse/TextBench/blob/main/elasticsearch/results/m6i.8xlarge_otel_logs_10b.json)** → **4\.13× faster in ClickHouse**
- **50B:** ClickHouse **[26\.2s](https://github.com/ClickHouse/TextBench/blob/main/clickhouse/results/m6i.8xlarge_50b.json)** vs Elasticsearch **[111\.4s](https://github.com/ClickHouse/TextBench/blob/main/elasticsearch/results/m6i.8xlarge_otel_logs_50b.json)** → **4\.25× faster in ClickHouse**

The next question is where that lead comes from, so we break the totals down by query shape.

### Cold runtime by query: where ClickHouse pulls ahead most [\#](/blog/elasticsearch-log-analytics-clickhouse#cold-runtime-by-query-where-clickhouse-pulls-ahead-most)

The per\-query breakdown shows that ClickHouse’s largest cold\-run gains appear on queries that combine full\-text filtering with heavier downstream aggregation work.

![Blog-FTS-vs-Elasticsearch.005.png](/uploads/Blog_FTS_vs_Elasticsearch_005_5ebb925312.png)

> Overall, the more the workload shifts from pure retrieval toward search \+ analytics, as it does in typical log analytics queries, the stronger ClickHouse’s advantage becomes.

- **Q8–Q9 trend\-analysis queries** show the widest gaps at larger scales, where many matching rows must be grouped into time buckets and counted.
- **Q6–Q7 service breakdown queries** also widen materially with scale, reflecting multi\-group aggregation over large result sets.
- **Q1–Q3 incident drill\-down queries** stay comparatively closer, as they are more retrieval\-oriented and involve less post\-filter aggregation.

That aligns with our earlier benchmarks (on [aggregations](https://clickhouse.com/blog/clickhouse_vs_elasticsearch_the_billion_row_matchup), and on [JSON analytics](https://clickhouse.com/blog/json-bench-clickhouse-vs-mongodb-elasticsearch-duckdb-postgresql)), where ClickHouse significantly outperformed Elasticsearch on large\-scale GROUP BY and count workloads. Those same aggregation strengths remain visible here once text matches are found.

Cold runs highlight storage efficiency and raw execution speed. But observability workloads also involve repeated queries over warm data, so we next compare hot runtimes.
