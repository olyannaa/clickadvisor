## The bigger picture [\#](/blog/elasticsearch-log-analytics-clickhouse#the-bigger-picture)

This benchmark completes the picture: [first](https://clickhouse.com/blog/clickhouse_vs_elasticsearch_the_billion_row_matchup), ClickHouse beat Elasticsearch on aggregations, [then](https://clickhouse.com/blog/json-bench-clickhouse-vs-mongodb-elasticsearch-duckdb-postgresql) on JSON analytics, and now on full\-text analytical search.

> ClickHouse now does search and still keeps its world\-class aggregation engine

ClickHouse full\-text search is purpose\-built for analytics and observability, enabling fast multi\-token search with aggregations over billions or trillions of rows.

This benchmark confirms it: across a realistic OpenTelemetry log workload of up to **50 billion rows**, ClickHouse consistently delivered the stronger overall result.

- **Far smaller storage footprint** — roughly **5× less disk space**, translating into **lower storage cost, lower query I/O, and more room for sustained ingest**.
- **Much faster cold queries** — **4× to 6× faster** when data had to be read from disk, which matters because **cold queries are routine in observability**.
- **Faster hot queries** — still roughly **1\.7× to 2\.6× faster** with warm caches, keeping **dashboards, drill\-down, and repeated analysis** responsive.

The widest gaps appeared on workloads that combined text search with grouping, counting, and time\-series breakdowns.

In observability, search is rarely the end of the query.
